Trade models

Capital vs bond

  • Start with a Melitz model
  • Make firm problem dynamic with one or more of:
    • Learning about trade costs
    • Learning about foreign demand (Eaton 2014, Rauch & Watson 2003)
    • Sunk entry cost (in addition to per period fixed operating costs)
    • Exporter productivity growth
    • Stochasic variable trade costs (Alessandria & Choi & Ruhl 2014:3 state Markov)
  • Add one of these at a time:
    • A domestic bond in each country with positive net supply. The government would be able to to set the supply directly (policy tool). Would need to think more about how to finance the government’s problem. We would probable want returns to be stochastic – maybe with stochastic government expenditures?
    • Household capital accumulation. Firms would rent from the HH
  • Questions:
    • What differences do these two savings mechanisms have on equilibrium?
    • Why do we want this to be in a trade model (i.e. what data can we look at for which this might be interesting)?
    • Are there any welfare implications of having the bond in fixed positive supply? Meaning this is another welfare tool the government could control

Stochastic volatility of random iceberg costs

  • 2 countries
  • Production setting (in each country)
    • Final goods firm. Inputs are differentiated varieties from home and foreign differentiated firms. Output is non-traded good that HH use for consumption and investment
    • Continuum of Differentiated goods firms
      • Use labor and capital (both supplied by domestic HH) to produce differentiated varieties.
      • Monopolistically competitive.
      • CRS technology
      • Goods are tradable, so firms have potential to earn domestic and foreign profits. CRS implies we can solve market by market
  • HH in each country
    • Representative consumer that only values consumption (inelastic labor)
    • Can save in captial (and risk free bond?)
  • Trade
    • Differentiated goods firms face fixed operating cost each period they sell into each market.
    • Also face a one time «sunk cost» for entering a market the first time (makes model dynamic)
    • Also face random iceberg cost, drawn from a particular distribution
  • Questions
    • Standard model would be to have the distribution of iceberg costs be degenerate
    • What if the distribution is i.i.d.?
    • What if drawn from a distribution D?
    • What if drawn from a distribution D_t, where variance of D_t is stochastic (perhaps AR(1))?
    • Any policy questions?

Alternative:

  • What if instead of stochastic trade costs we had that differentiated firm productivity is stochastic?

Within firm trade

  • Empirical facts (ask Kim for more info):
    • More than 1⁄2 of international trade is within firms (domestic parent trading with foreign children)
    • Most international parent-child pairs don’t trade with one another.
    • This means that there are relatively few large firms trading a lot with foreign relatives.
  • Questions
    • What kind of model could explain this? Some particular structure of trade costs?
